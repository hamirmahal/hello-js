## JS Basics (Part III)
### Literals
Literals are fixed values that you provide in your script. There are 7 different types of literals:
  1. [Array literals](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/JavaScript/Guide/Grammar_and_types#Array_literals)
  2. [Booleans literals](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/JavaScript/Guide/Grammar_and_types#Boolean_literals)
  3. [Floating-point literals](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/JavaScript/Guide/Grammar_and_types#Floating-point_literals)
  4. [Numeric literals](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/JavaScript/Guide/Grammar_and_types#Numeric_literals)
  5. [Object literals](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/JavaScript/Guide/Grammar_and_types#Object_literals)
  6. [RegExp literals](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/JavaScript/Guide/Grammar_and_types#RegExp_literals)
  7. [String literals](https://developer.mozilla.org/en-US/docs/Web/JavaScript/Guide/Grammar_and_types#String_literals)
